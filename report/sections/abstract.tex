\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Recurring Glacial Lake Outburst Floods (GLOFs) from the Plaine Morte glacier have been
documented in the Simme catchment since 2011. We investigate whether these events
systematically distort the flood frequency statistics derived from the annual maximum
discharge series (AMS) at GRDC station 6935331 (Simme at Oberried/Lenk, 1944--2020).
Three Generalised Extreme Value (GEV) distributions are fitted by maximum likelihood to
(i) the full 1944--2020 series, (ii) a GLOF-masked variant, and (iii) a pre-GLOF
baseline (1944--2010). Two complementary hypothesis tests --- a Kolmogorov--Smirnov
goodness-of-fit test and a non-parametric Rank-Sum test --- confirm that the shift in
extreme-value statistics observed after 2010 is attributable to the GLOF signal rather
than to secular hydroclimatic change. After masking, the GLOF-era maxima are
statistically indistinguishable from the pre-GLOF reference distribution, and engineering
design floods for return periods up to 200 years are reduced by 5--9\,\%.
\end{abstract}
