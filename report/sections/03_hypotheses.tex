\section{Hypotheses}
\label{sec:hyp}

\textbf{H1 (GLOF inflation):} Fitting a GEV distribution to the full annual maximum
series (1974--2025 for BAFU~2219; 1944--2020 for GRDC) produces significantly larger
design quantiles than fitting to the pre-GLOF reference period (before 2011), and the
GLOF-inclusive 2011--2018 peaks are statistically incompatible with the reference
distribution.

\textbf{H2 (Masking restores homogeneity):} After replacing the GLOF-peak contribution
with a $\pm 5$-day masked annual maximum, the 2011--2018 peaks are no longer
statistically distinguishable from the pre-GLOF reference distribution. This would imply
that the underlying natural-flood regime of the Simme has remained stationary, and that
the observed shift in extreme-value statistics is attributable solely to the direct GLOF
signal rather than to broader hydroclimatic change.
