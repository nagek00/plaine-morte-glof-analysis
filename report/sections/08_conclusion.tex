\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This study demonstrates that the seven GLOFs from Plaine Morte glacier documented between
2011 and 2018 significantly distort flood frequency statistics at the Simme gauge in Lenk.
The key findings are:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Statistically detectable GLOF signal.}
        Both the KS test ($D_n = 0.481 > C_{0.05} = 0.433$) and the Rank-Sum test
        ($p = 0.032$) reject the null hypothesis that GLOF-inclusive 2011--2020 peaks are
        consistent with the pre-GLOF reference distribution.

  \item \textbf{Masking restores homogeneity.}
        After applying a $\pm 5$-day GLOF masking window, neither test rejects $H_0$
        (KS: $D_n = 0.274$, $p = 0.498$; Rank-Sum: $p = 0.412$). The masked era is
        statistically indistinguishable from the 1944--2010 baseline.

  \item \textbf{Design flood inflation.}
        Unmasked GEV fitting overstates the 100-year and 200-year design quantiles by
        approximately 8--12\,\% relative to the GLOF-masked or pre-GLOF reference fit.

  \item \textbf{No secular trend.}
        There is no evidence of hydroclimatic change in the non-GLOF portion of the
        post-2010 record (Rank-Sum 5c: $p = 0.81$), supporting the use of the full
        1944--2020 masked series for a stationary flood frequency analysis.
\end{enumerate}

For engineering applications, we recommend using the GLOF-masked or pre-GLOF reference GEV
as the design distribution for natural flood hazard assessment, while treating the GLOF
contribution as a separate, superimposed hazard to be quantified through event-based GLOF
routing rather than merged into the AMS.
